Frequently Asked Questions

  • Solar energy refers to the radiant light and heat emitted by the Sun, which can be captured and utilized through various technologies. These include solar heating, photovoltaics (solar panels), solar thermal energy systems, solar architecture, molten salt power plants, and artificial photosynthesis.

    As a vital source of renewable energy, solar energy plays a significant role in addressing energy needs while reducing reliance on fossil fuels. The technologies that harness solar energy can be categorized into two main types: passive solar systems, which naturally collect and distribute sunlight without mechanical assistance, and active solar systems, which actively convert sunlight into usable power through various processes.

  • Solar panels can generate electricity in varying sunlight conditions, but they perform best in direct sunlight. Areas with an average of 4-7 sunlight hours per day are generally suitable for solar panel installations.
